The FTSE 100 closed up 0.1 per cent, while the pound added 0.3 per cent against the dollar and 0.6 per cent against the euro as Britain moved one step closer to leave the European Union after MPs passed the withdrawal agreement bill today.

Meanwhile, former Bank of England deputy governor Andrew Bailey, who is currently chief executive of the Financial Conduct Authority, has been named as the new governor of the British central bank.

In company news, Shell shares fell 1.6 per cent as the oil giant trimmed its forecast for quarterly oil production sales.
