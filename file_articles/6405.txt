BUCHAREST, Dec 19 (Reuters) - Here are news stories, press reports and events to watch which may affect Romanian financial markets on Thursday.

DEBT TENDER

Romanian debt managers tender 500 million lei of one-year treasury bills and 800 million lei of June 2024 treasury bonds.

On Wednesday, they sold a more than planned 416 million euros ($458.52 million) of 2021 bonds at an average yield of 0.13% and rejected all bids at a tender to sell 2025 euro paper, central bank data showed.

2020 BUDGET

Romania's government approved a 2020 budget bill on Wednesday with a deficit target of 3.6% of gross domestic product, down from this year's estimated 4.4%, and said it would fast-track its approval in parliament before the end of December.

PIPELINE

The Ukrainian and Romanian national gas pipeline operators have signed an interconnection agreement to allow gas supplies from Turkey and southern Europe to Ukraine and Moldova, the Ukrainian company said on Wednesday.

CEE MARKETS

Currencies in Eastern Europe fell slightly on Wednesday, as investors faced fresh Brexit fears ahead of a Czech central bank policy meeting where rates are set to remain unchanged despite inflation concerns.
